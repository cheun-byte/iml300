Kishu Kisses

small little memories,
bursting with sweetness and flavor
i savored what few seasons it felt like i had with you.

i’m still a sticky-fingered child,
peeling apart a mandarin with wild abandon
but you are no longer here to help me delicately pry the fruit apart,
your palms no longer guiding my hands.

kishus aren’t as sweet as they used to be,
but i think it’s because i can no longer share these moments
with you.
