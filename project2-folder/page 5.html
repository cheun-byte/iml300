Summertime Feelings, Ocean Song

Summertime just has that feeling.

That peel-y feeling of a sunkissed nose, the sand between your toes, that feeling of laughter and pure sunshine. Like the first sip of a cool lemonade as sweat drip, drip, drips down your back. That first leap into cold, salty water that burns oh-so-good in your eyes and leaves the gritty, bitter taste of sand on your tongue. The sounds of crashing, rolling waves are an unheard symphony to those who don’t understand, who probably never will, how the ocean sings.

The song of the sea hums a tune of constant change. A never paused state of existence that goes this-way then that-way, a chorus of indecision. A voice of changing tides and uncertain winds, a voice that calls out for me to come home. Home to the million pieces of sand stuck to my burnt legs, of cold and slimy seaweed, of shining sea glass and glowing algae.

Oh, how the water sings for summer.

How pitiful it must be for that song to fall on deaf ears, to eternally attempt to call for those who have never learned to yearn for the sea.
