Paper and Ink

The first thing I remember
Is glue being brushed against my spine, sticking me between the confines
Of paper, or leather, or some other decorative cage which will bind me.
A confined amount of space, a limited number of pages,
But the possibility of infinite creation and unending thought
Will be inked into my skin.

Perhaps I will be adopted by an artist, filled with snapshots and ideas haphazardly
Scribbled into my folds, covered in an array of colors and textures,
The testing grounds for new found combinations, with
Paints and markers and watercolor sinking past every layer of my body.

Or maybe a writer will pick me up, select me as the storage for every pent up draft
Which screams in their head, a torrent of voices, falling silent once written down.
Diagrams and plots unveiled in their minds, granting them clarity from the
Weight of building new worlds and restoring old ones.

Maybe someone finally decides to give journaling a try,
Allowing me to witness every wrinkle in their brain
Without the pressure of an audience.
Tears will stain my tattoos, warping each word,
But at least I’ve helped someone begin to truly know their own self.

Despite the fact many find comfort in my confines,
Reassured by the fact that I have a tangible beginning and end,
Whether I am carried everywhere in a bag
Or forgotten in a drawer alongside my brethren,
I relish in the responsibility of safekeeping their thoughts, their desires,
The pieces of themselves they find so important, so noteworthy,
It must be written down.
