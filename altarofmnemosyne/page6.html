Helios

To me, he is the sun.

Brightening my day, making me smile from his warmth,
His words never fail to lift me like the clouds on a clear day.

He is Apollo, his voice a pure symphony.
Modeled to embody the healing warmth of fanned sun rays,
Or perhaps the one tune you hum under your breath
That you never seem to forget.

When he is gone life seems like a silent, moonless night.
Not even a soft illumination remains,
Just time passing slowly until he rises again.

If he is the sun, then I must be Icarus.

Wishing to be closer, to stand alongside him,
But my hubris is not what weighs me down.
It is my timidity,
My fear for someone to see the deepest, inner parts of me,
And turn away.

Because how could someone like me,
Be worthy of someone like him?
He is a shining star, a perfect god,
And I am nothing but a mortal with wings of wax.

If it is not his flaming heat that consumes me first,
Then the weight of my flaws will.

And yet I am painfully, blissfully aware of this.

That if I come too close I will burn.
That the wax that holds my feathered flesh together will melt,
That I will fall,
That my body and soul will splinter into the pieces of a forgotten carcass,
Just for me to mend my heart and wings to soar once again,
In search of a new flaming star.

Perhaps I am Daedalus,
Knowing that no matter how hard I try to warn myself
Of the dangers that come with wings,
I will inevitably fall time and time again.
